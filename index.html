Australia's Tourism Comeback

Where visitors are coming from, why they are travelling, and which airports are carrying the recovery.

This visualisation explores how international arrivals, visitor purpose, source countries, seasonality and airport traffic have changed across three decades of Australian travel.

Section 1

Australia’s Open Door

Australia’s visitor economy has grown over the long term, but the timeline also shows how global shocks interrupted travel before the sector began recovering.

<

Total international arrivals, 1991–2025

An annual timeline shows long-term growth, COVID-era disruption and the recovery of international arrivals after borders reopened.

Purpose of visit over time

A stacked area chart shows how the mix of holiday, business, education and family travel has shifted over time.

Section 2

Who Comes to Australia?

Source countries reveal the changing shape of Australian tourism and the growing importance of nearby Asia-Pacific visitor markets.

World map of source countries

A choropleth map shows which countries send the most visitors to Australia, with darker shading representing higher arrivals in 2025.

Top 10 source countries

A lollipop chart ranks Australia’s largest visitor markets in 2025, showing New Zealand and China as the two biggest sources.

The rise of Asia

A slope chart compares regional visitor shares between 2000 and 2025, showing Asia’s growing share of Australia’s visitor market.

Section 3

Why Do They Come?

Visitor purpose and length of stay help explain whether Australia is attracting short holiday trips, family visits, students, business travellers or longer stays.

Main reason for journey

A 100% stacked bar chart compares visitor purpose across selected years, making it easier to see how the mix of holiday, business, education and family travel has changed.

How long visitors stay

A duration distribution shows whether visitors in 2025 are mostly short-stay or long-stay travellers.

Section 4

Where Does the Recovery Land?

Airport traffic is used as a supporting indicator of where travel activity is concentrated across Australia.

Passenger volume by Australian airport

A bubble map uses circle size to show total passengers in 2024 and colour to show each airport’s international passenger share.

Domestic vs international mix

A proportional stacked bar shows which airports are mostly domestic and which have a stronger international passenger role in 2024.

Airport recovery comparison

A recovery index compares 2024 passenger traffic against 2019 levels, where 100% means an airport has returned to its pre-pandemic passenger volume.

Section 5

Recovery Patterns and Seasonality

Monthly patterns show that tourism recovery is not only about total volume, but also timing, seasonality and different airport trajectories.

Seasonality heatmap

A month-by-year heatmap shows normal tourism seasonality, the COVID-era interruption and the return of visitor arrivals after borders reopened.

Top airport passenger trends

A multi-line chart compares monthly traffic trends for five major airports, showing the drop during border closures and the uneven recovery after travel resumed.

Data sources
